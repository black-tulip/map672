The passage from Pickles is primarily a plea for self awareness by the cartographer. At this point though this plea is almost a cliche. We now firmly grasp that a mapping is never mirroring (indeed even the smoothest mirror distorts); that mapping can be an exercise in power; that it will have unintended consequenses; and that any claim by a cartographer to show all information equally over a mapped space is by definition false. As the floating sheep manifesto put it, "all maps lie". So what should we do about it? Well in a way, all disciplines that show us the world, from academic research to journalism, struggle with this tension. What appealed to me about the manifesto is that it starts to give us a set of principles that apply to mapping as it is today.
